Jiang Juan was extremely anxious about Qian Liang's misfortune and knew that Qian Liang was innocent, but—

he was taken away.

In the guest room, Jiang Juan was placed on the bed. Seeing that she was no longer needed, the maid quietly retreated, closing the door softly with a "creak."

After a short sound, the room fell silent. For some reason, Jiang Juan suddenly felt tense.

He shouldn't have looked around or thought too much.

Otherwise, he wouldn't feel awkward seeing the Prince now.

Jiang Juan was very annoyed. His pale fingertips suddenly touched his forehead. Xue Fangli lowered his head and asked, "What's wrong?"

Shaking his head randomly, Jiang Juan's eyes wandered, not daring to meet Xue Fangli's gaze. "Nothing."

After saying that, he added, "Prince, I really have nothing."

Xue Fangli kept watching him. Jiang Juan's discomfort was noted in his eyes, but he didn't react much. Instead, he said calmly, "Let the physician see you first; take some rest."

No matter what, Jiang Juan could only accept it. He replied with an "oh," climbed onto the bed, and lay flat.

But a few seconds later, Jiang Juan sat up again.

"…It's too hard."

Jiang Juan reached out to touch the sore spot on his back.

At Liang Wangfu, knowing how precious he was, Xue Fangli had several layers of soft padding laid everywhere he sat or slept so that Jiang Juan wouldn't complain about pain.

But this was General Mansion. General Jiang wasn't someone who enjoyed luxuries; not only were there no soft pads, but the beds in the general's mansion were even harder than elsewhere. Jiang Juan couldn't get used to sleeping on them.

Xue Fangli asked him, "Hold you?"

Not that it wasn't possible, there was nothing to stop it either. The Prince was comfortable to sit and sleep on, but Jiang Juan went through a series of mental preparations before slowly saying, "Maybe... let's not?"

The Prince hugging him merely meant letting Jiang Juan lie in his arms. Jiang Juan had always slept happily and comfortably before, but now—

The grotesque scenes from the book flooded into his mind again, refusing to dissipate. He sighed hopelessly and muttered to himself, "Actually, the bed isn't that hard; I can still sleep if I endure it for a while."

"Prince, don't bother with me."

After saying this, Jiang Juan leaned back again.

He endured it once, twice, three times...

Jiang Juan couldn't take it anymore. The bed was really too hard, and the discomfort was unbearable. He started tossing and turning on the bed as if lying not on a bed but in a pan of hot oil, burning him so much that he kept flipping over.

After staring at him for a while, Xue Fangli reached out to embrace him, but Jiang Juan instinctively pushed him away. However, Xue Fangli firmly pressed him into his arms with a dark look in his eyes, "If you can't get used to sleeping like this, why not let me hold you?"

"Are you still upset that this King kissed you?"

Jiang Juan: "Ah?"

He slightly opened his eyes and after a while of staring blankly, shook his head and said, "... No."

Xue Fangli asked him emotionally, "Then why won't you let this King hold you again?"

It wasn't that he refused; it just felt awkward.

How should he explain?

Jiang Juan didn't bother explaining to the Prince. After all, if he had to explain properly, he would have to start from when he picked up the book "Taiping Youji." But that was—

How embarrassing? Jiang Juan felt a bit helpless. "I didn't say you couldn't hug me," Jiang Juan said sheepishly, "aren't you hugging me right now?" Xue Fangli did not respond, only staring at Jiang Juan intently, clearly disbelieving his explanation. Jiang Juan was in a dilemma. If he told the truth, he would feel embarrassed. But if he didn't, Prince Wang seemed to have misunderstood. "Don't be like her... Jiang Qingmei, hating this prince yet constantly summoning him by your side." Why was she bringing up the Lady Yu Mei again? Jiang Juan wanted to stop her, but before he could speak, Xue Fangli continued in a cold tone, "She always summons this prince to her side, eager to play the role of an ordinary mother in this world, pouring tea for this prince, preparing meals, yet she still poisons his tea and puts blood in his food." "Prince Wang..." Jiang Juan was taken aback. Every time Prince Wang mentioned Lady Yu Mei, it wasn't good news. What was even more terrifying was that Prince Wang had told him many things about her over the years, but there were always aspects Jiang Juan didn't know, each time resulting in Lady Yu Mei torturing Prince Wang. Jiang Juan gradually learned more about Prince Wang's past, but he felt it was only the tip of the iceberg; Prince Wang had endured far more hardships than what he had openly said. Therefore, every time Prince Wang mentioned Lady Yu Mei, Jiang Juan would feel soft-hearted. This time was no exception, though this time Jiang Juan also felt a bit guilty. If not for him, Prince Wang wouldn't have been so suspicious.

Jiang Juan: "It's not that I don't like it..."

Xue Fangli: "Why won't you let me hold you?"

After going around in circles, they ended up back at this question.

He had to be more honest.

He couldn't let the Prince regale in wild thoughts.

Isn't it just embarrassing?

Jiang Juan comforted herself and mustered up the courage to say, "Prince..."

Xue Fangli lowered his eyes and met his gaze. Jiang Juan found himself involuntarily recalling a painting—ambiguous postures, familiar scenes—one day, he had grazed the inside of his thigh while riding a horse, and the Prince had treated him in the same way.

Jiang Juan: "... "

Xue Fangli: "You're not annoying. Why are you crying? And why won't you let me hold you?"

Jiang Juan sighed in resignation, his gaze darting around aimlessly. In desperation, he blurted out a random reason, "Why are you asking me? We said no kissing, you gave me a bite, but you bit me many times and even kissed me again at the end."

"You're being so unreasonable. Why should I cry or refuse your hug?"

After all, it was just an excuse, Jiang Juan didn't say it with confidence. While complaining to Xue Fangli, he secretly tried to crawl out of his embrace, but before long, his ankle was grabbed, pulling him back. Xue Fangli pressed Jiang Juan into his arms again, "Yes, I was too unreasonable."

Jiang Juan lowered his head and looked at the hand that restrained him in his arms, sighing gloomily, "If you're being so unreasonable, why don't you reflect on it properly? Why bring me back?"

Xue Fangli looked at him, "To make up for it."

Jiang Juan blinked, "How are you going to compensate?"

Jiang Juan: "It's not that I don't like it..."

Xue Fangli: "Why won't you let me hold you?"

The very book Juan Ge had touched earlier.

Xue Fangli had returned, and how long he had been away was unclear. He looked at Jiang Qingliang expressionlessly for a moment, then his gaze shifted downward to land on the "Spring Palace Picture Album" in his hand.

Jiang Qingliang: "..."

Seeing Xue Fangli like this made his fingers tremble uncontrollably. Recalling what he had said, Jiang Qingliang's hair stood on end. He could only pray in his heart that Xue Fangli had arrived late and hadn't stayed too long.

But heaven did not grant his wish. Seeing that he still hadn't left, Jiang Juan emphasized again, "You were told Prince Wang can do it, so why haven't you gone?"

Jiang Qingliang's heart was like a dead ember.

Immediately after, Jiang Juan was also bewildered.

"Thank Your Excellency for clarifying this matter."
