It seemed like a long time had passed, yet it felt like just an instant.

The long night gradually brightened as consciousness began to return. Jiang Juan faced the glaring lights, everything bathed in brightness akin to daylight.

"Beep, beep, beep—"

Instruments emitted urgent and sharp sounds. Jiang Juan heard several voices.

"Teacher, he’s bleeding again! The bleeding won’t stop, it simply can’t be stopped!"

"Heart rate is irregular, the patient's heart rate is too low!"

"Teacher—he has stopped beating!"

Jiang Juan struggled to open his eyes, but he had no strength at all. He clearly felt life force draining away. Jiang Juan felt tired and cold.

It’s better to keep sleeping.

When asleep, I won’t feel tired, and I won’t feel cold.

Thinking this way, Jiang Juan gave up resistance.

Those sounds—the beeping of instruments, anxious cries, and hurried footsteps—faded from his ears, and Jiang Juan once again fell into darkness.

Consciousness scattered in the silence.

Floating.

"You have an extremely good life force; you can be called a prosperous soul. It’s just that your destiny is fated with three calamities."

His calamities.

Think quickly.

Faster.

The sound of hurried footsteps and the rapid beeping of instruments echoed in his ears again. Jiang Juan heard many people speaking to him.

"Don't sleep."

"Hang on a bit longer, just a bit more."

Don't sleep.

He still couldn't remember anything; nothing came back to him.

He couldn't afford to sleep.

He had to recall what he had forgotten, because the things he forgot were truly important.

Jiang Juan repeatedly told himself this and struggled desperately.

After an unknown duration of time, in the endless darkness, faint glimmers began to appear. They danced continuously until they coalesced into a single beam, and Jiang Juan saw light once more.

A trembling, bright light.

At that very moment, Jiang Juan finally remembered what he had forgotten.

He promised someone he would not leave him.

He promised Xue Fangli that he would not leave him.

"The surgery was successful."

This was the last thing Jiang Juan heard before she lost consciousness.

When she woke up again, it was ten days later.

Jiang Juan sat up dazedly. She tried to recognize her surroundings, confirming that this was a completely unfamiliar place. She touched her heart, finding no trace of any surgical incision.

Was it all just a dream?

She returned to the operating table. The situation was still critical, but the surgery was successful in the end.

"You're awake."

With a creak, someone opened the door. An old man wearing tattered robes walked in with his hands behind his back, swaying slightly. Jiang Juan looked up and immediately recognized him.

"Master Ananda..."

Ananda waved her hand and asked Jiang Juan, "How do you feel?"

Jiang Juan hesitated, "Alright."

Ananda nodded, "That's good then."

Jiang Juan had many questions for him but didn't know where to start, so she fell silent.

Ananda gazed at Jiang Juan. There was no longer the shrewdness of a fraudster who asked her to pay money to avoid disaster on his face last time. Instead, his eyes were filled with all-seeing wisdom. Sarira?

Jiang Juan understood. He looked at what was on his wrist and suddenly didn't want to touch it anymore.

Anan continued mumbling, "People can still reason, what is this thing? Reason doesn't work with him, he kills at a whim, just like a ghost, even Buddha would be worried seeing him."

Jiang Juan: "..."

Just mentioning the thing that makes even Buddha worry made Anan's face turn green. He seriously said to Jiang Juan, "Keep him in check. In the future, you must properly suppress evil and don't let him cause trouble everywhere again."

Jiang Juan could only respond, "I'll try my best."

Since all that needed to be said was said, Anan waved his sleeves like shooing away flies and said, "Get up and leave quickly when you wake up. If you delay any longer, he might think you're still asleep and destroy my humble abode again."

Jiang Juan felt a bit embarrassed, "I'm sorry..."

Knowing that what Anan said was right, if he delayed further, Xue Fangli might really cause trouble for someone else, Jiang Juan got out of bed.

He supported himself against the wall and took a few steps. Suddenly, Jiang Juan remembered something and asked Anan, "Since I stayed here, where is my home?"

"I read a book, then I ended up in this world. Is all of this just a dream of mine? Or is it... something that really exists?"
