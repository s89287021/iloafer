Jiang Juan's injured knee happened to be on the leg adorned with a small-leaf purple sandalwood Buddha bead. His ankle was grabbed by a hand, and his movement ceased. Only this string of beads swung incessantly at his ankle.

The dark wooden beads contrasted sharply with his fair skin, fitting Jiang Juan perfectly in their purity and dustlessness. Yet, they did not quite match him; the beads were slightly larger, as was the bracelet, silently declaring a certain sentiment.

Scholar Xue Fangli watched as he grabbed Jiang Juan's ankle with one hand, stroking the beads. His fingers moved slowly over them, gently rolling the beads.

He was touching small-leaf purple sandalwood beads, but Jiang Juan felt itchy, his jade-like toes curling uncontrollably. He tried to retreat, but his ankle was gripped too tightly, and he could not pull away.

"Let go," he said softly.

Jiang Juan murmured, "This is so strange."

Scholar Xue Fangli asked, "What's strange?"

His tone was calm, and even as he spoke, he continued to stroke the beads, seemingly playing with them slowly. Yet, what he was really playing with didn't seem to be the beads; his gaze remained fixed on Jiang Juan's curled toes.

Jiang Juan replied, "Everything is strange."

Scholar Xue Fangli asked, "Then how about this?"

His fingers slipped into the beads and rested on the ankle bone on the outer side of Jiang Juan's wrist.

The tip of his finger was slightly cold, but the moment it touched Jiang Juan, he felt a wave of heat. His reaction was like being scalded; he suddenly retracted, only to accidentally step into a basin of water placed on the floor with a splash.

"Slurp," water splashed everywhere, wetting Jiang Juan's leg and Scholar Xue Fangli's sleeves. At that moment, everything was silent except for the sound of water.

He was not in good health and looked forward to the New Year's Eve fireworks every year, wanting to go to the scene, but the night of the new year was always crowded and bustling, and his heart was too fragile. So he could only watch them on live stream.

The fireworks lasted for quite a while, as did Jiang Juan's gaze fixed on the falling tails. He still felt somewhat disappointed, leaning despondently against the window, asking Xue Fangli, "Your Highness, are we back at the Wangfu now?"

"It's so high."

Jiang Juan only wanted to watch the fireworks and had no intention of climbing up the tower. He earnestly said, "Young Master, you can just watch from inside the carriage; there's no need to go up there."

Xue Fangli was unmoved, "Get down."

After hesitating for a long time, Jiang Juan reluctantly held out his hand as Xue Fangli reached for him. Slowly, he stepped out of the carriage, but before his foot could touch the ground, Xue Fangli suddenly pulled him in.

He was dragged into his arms.

Jiang Juan was taken aback and quickly hugged him tightly.

It was nice to avoid work, but the Phoenix Tower was too tall. Jiang Juan was a bit scared and said anxiously, "Young Master, put me down; I want to walk by myself."

Xue Fangli stopped in his tracks, and with a half-smile that was neither a smile nor a frown, he asked him, "If I can't do it, can you?"

In any case, Jiang Juan felt that he could do better than the Prince. However, realizing that his words earlier might hurt the Prince's pride, Jiang Juan hurriedly said, "Your Highness, you can do it, you're the best."

Xue Fangli lowered his eyes and saw Jiang Juan huddled in his arms, almost too scared to look down. He still nodded and put him down.

They didn't reach the very top but had climbed halfway up. Jiang Juan sat on the stairs rather carelessly and invited Xue Fangli to join him, "Your Highness, sit here."

Xue Fangli glanced at him, lifted his robe, and sat down. Although he was sitting directly on the ground, his posture remained exquisitely elegant.

At this moment, the evening breeze was gentle, and the night sky was as calm as water.

Jiang Juan looked up at the sky and uncertainly asked Xue Fangli, "Your Highness, will there really be fireworks?"

Xue Fangli nodded, "Yeah."

His certainty convinced Jiang Juan. He looked left and right, about to say something to Xue Fangli, when a loud boom echoed, and the fireworks soared into the air, slowly blossoming and scattering a carpet of starlight.

"Boom boom boom—!"

Jiang Juan lifted his head and stared for a long time without blinking, suddenly remembering something he had seen before. He took out a scroll from his sleeve.

"Your Highness, here it is."

Xue Fangli made no movement; he simply stared at Jiang Juan.

In the flickering light of the fireworks, the young man's face alternated between bright and dim. He smiled with his eyebrows curved upward, and his soft lips also curled slightly, his eyes clear and pure.

Jiang Juan asked him, "Your Highness, what is it?"

Xue Fangli said, "You..."

He opened his mouth to say a word, but it came out in a hoarse voice, and he didn't know what else to say.

The young man had asked for the painting to repair an old one for him.

Jiang Juan agreed to paint because of that same old painting.

It was for him, yet also because of him.

Xue Fangli stared at Jiang Juan. He recalled many times when he had been tempted and many times when he had restrained himself.

A single moment of desire had stirred his thoughts.

If he hadn't indulged in those desires, how could he have lived day after day with constant anxiety, suspicion, and looking over his shoulder?

If he had not been moved, how would he have wanted the youth to feel pity for him? How could he bear to see the youth too deeply affected and try to make the youth fear him? And what if the youth truly did fear him, unwilling even to bury his head in his arms again?

He had long harbored such thoughts and been moved.

Perhaps it was the youth's smile, suggesting that he was good, or perhaps it was the youth sobbing senselessly in his arms while trying to comfort himself, or perhaps it was when he gave him an oracle slip at the Bodhimanda...

It was his carefulness, his self-restraint, and ultimately—

he could no longer control his emotions.

At this moment, the long-suppressed, deepest desires hidden in his heart finally broke through the dam and surged forth.

He wanted many things, yet he also wanted very little.

He wanted Jiang Juan to be filled with thoughts of him, for all of her world to belong to him, and he only desired Jiang Juan.

After an indeterminate amount of time, Xue Fangli finally spoke. He looked at Jiang Juan, his expression unreadable, but his voice was soft and gentle.

"From now on, this king will only care about you. What do you think?"
